# BBQ (Barbecue)

![BBQ spread](/images/bbq-hero.jpg)

## Flavor Profile

American BBQ is about **low and slow** cooking with smoke. Regional styles vary dramatically in meat choice, wood type, and sauce preferences.

The key is **patience**â€”meats are smoked for hours until tender, developing a **smoke ring** and caramelized exterior called **bark**.

## ðŸŸ¢ Beginner Dishes

### Pulled Pork
Slow-smoked pork shoulder, shredded and often served with sauce.

**What to expect:** Tender, smoky, slightly sweet (especially Carolina style)

---

### Brisket (Lean)
Smoked beef brisket sliced thin. Texas specialty.

**What to expect:** Smoky, beefy flavor, tender but not fatty

---

### BBQ Chicken
Smoked or grilled chicken with BBQ sauce.

**What to expect:** Familiar chicken with sweet-tangy sauce

## ðŸŸ¡ Intermediate Dishes

### Ribs (Baby Back or Spare)
Pork ribs smoked until meat pulls cleanly from bone.

**What to expect:** Fall-off-the-bone tender, smoky with sweet or tangy sauce

---

### Brisket (Fatty)
The "point" cut of brisket with more marbling and bark.

**What to expect:** Rich, unctuous, intense smoke flavor

---

### Burnt Ends
Caramelized, cubed brisket point. Kansas City specialty.

**What to expect:** Candy-like bark, intense flavor, fatty and tender

## Regional Styles

### Texas BBQ
Focus: **Brisket**  
Sauce: Minimal or noneâ€”meat speaks for itself  
Wood: Oak or mesquite

### Kansas City BBQ
Focus: **Everything** (ribs, burnt ends, pulled pork)  
Sauce: Thick, sweet, tomato-based  
Style: Heavy smoke ring

### Carolina BBQ
Focus: **Pulled pork**  
Sauce: Vinegar-based (Eastern) or mustard-based (South Carolina)  
Style: Whole hog cooking

### Memphis BBQ
Focus: **Ribs**  
Sauce: Thin, tangy, tomato-based  
Style: Dry rub vs wet ribs
