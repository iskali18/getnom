# Japanese Cuisine

![Japanese food spread](/images/japanese-hero.jpg)

## Flavor Profile

Japanese cuisine emphasizes **freshness, seasonality, and subtle flavors**. It focuses on bringing out the natural taste of ingredients rather than heavy sauces or spices.

Key elements include **umami** (from soy sauce, miso, dashi), **delicate preparation**, and **beautiful presentation**. Rice and fish are central to most meals.

## ðŸŸ¢ Beginner Dishes

### Chicken Teriyaki
Grilled chicken with sweet soy-based glaze. Mild and accessible.

**What to expect:** Sweet-savory flavor, tender chicken

---

### California Roll
Sushi roll with crab (or imitation crab), avocado, and cucumber. Rice on the outside.

**What to expect:** Mild, creamy, no raw fish

---

### Chicken Katsu
Breaded, fried chicken cutlet served with tangy sauce. Crispy and comforting.

**What to expect:** Crunchy breading, juicy chicken, similar to schnitzel

---

## ðŸŸ¡ Intermediate Dishes

### Ramen
Noodle soup with rich broth (tonkotsu, miso, or shoyu), pork, egg, and vegetables.

**What to expect:** Hearty, savory broth with chewy noodles

---

### Salmon Nigiri
Hand-pressed sushi with raw salmon over vinegared rice.

**What to expect:** Clean, buttery fish flavor, cool temperature

---

### Gyoza
Pan-fried dumplings filled with pork and vegetables.

**What to expect:** Crispy bottom, juicy filling, served with dipping sauce

---

## ðŸ”´ Adventurous Dishes

### Uni (Sea Urchin)
Creamy, briny seafood delicacy. Served as sushi or sashimi.

**What to expect:** Custard-like texture, ocean flavor (love it or hate it)

---

### Natto
Fermented soybeans with sticky texture and strong smell.

**What to expect:** Pungent, acquired taste, slimy texture

---

### Fugu (Pufferfish)
Carefully prepared delicacy (contains toxins if not prepared correctly).

**What to expect:** Mild, delicate flavor, slight tingle on tongue
